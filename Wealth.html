Wealth





Elon Musk's net worth has seen significant fluctuations over the years. He made $175.8 million from the sale of PayPal to eBay in 2002 and first appeared on the Forbes Billionaires List in 2012 with a net worth of $2 billion.
His wealth soared in 2020, reaching $185 billion in January 2021, making him the world's richest person, surpassing Jeff Bezos. By November 2021, his net worth crossed $300 billion, but by December 2022, he experienced
the largest loss of personal fortune in history, losing $200 billion due to declining Tesla stock. The majority of Musk's wealth, around 75% in November 2020, came from Tesla stock, but this proportion decreased to about 37%
by December 2022 after selling nearly $40 billion in company shares since late 2021. Musk doesn't receive a salary from Tesla; instead, his compensation is tied to the company's valuation and revenue, and he only receives it
if specific market values are reached. Between 2014 and 2018, Musk paid $455 million in taxes on $1.52 billion of income, but according to ProPublica, he paid no federal income taxes in 2018. He claimed his 2021 tax
bill was estimated at $12 billion based on the sale of $14 billion worth of Tesla stock. Musk has mentioned that he considers himself "cash poor" and isn't interested in material possessions. He has pledged to sell most
of his physical possessions and has defended his wealth by stating that he is accumulating resources for humanity's expansion into space.
