Business Career





Elon Musk is a business magnate and investor known for his involvement in various companies and ventures. He co-founded Zip2, an Internet city guide software company, which was later acquired by Compaq for $307 million.
He also co-founded X.com, which merged with Confinity to become PayPal, acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion. Musk founded SpaceX, a spaceflight services company, and developed the Starlink constellation for satellite Internet
access. Musk joined Tesla, Inc. as CEO and product architect after leading an investment round and has played a significant role in the development of electric vehicles. He also provided funding for SolarCity, later acquired by
Tesla to become Tesla Energy. Musk co-founded Neuralink, a neurotechnology startup, and founded The Boring Company to construct tunnels for high-speed transport. In 2022, Musk purchased Twitter for $44 billion and
took on the role of CEO. He launched xAI, an AI company, in 2023. Musk's leadership style is characterized as micromanaging and demanding long working hours, with a mix of praise and impulsiveness in decision-making.
Throughout his career, Musk has faced legal challenges, including a lawsuit from the SEC over tweets about Tesla and PayPal, as well as shareholder lawsuits. He has also faced criticism for making unscientific and misleading
statements, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, and has been involved in controversies related to his companies' activities, including environmental concerns and animal welfare violations. Despite this, he is one
of the wealthiest individuals globally, with an estimated net worth of over $239 billion as of July 2023.
